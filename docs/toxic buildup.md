### **Toxic buildup**

### Primarily occurs with exposure to [toxic fallout](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Toxic_fallout), [pollution](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Pollution), and [tox gas](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Tox_gas). Prolonged exposure gradually increases the buildup severity.

Alternatively, some attacks cause instantaneous increases in toxic buildup severity, such as [cobra](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Cobra) and [waste rat](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Waste_rat) bites, the [venom talon](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Venom_talon) , or [venom fangs](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Venom_fangs) .

A colonist under a roof is protected from toxic fallout, and avoiding interaction with the other sources can prevent buildup from them. If they are exposed to the fallout, they will accumulate Toxic Buildup at a rate of 40% per day. Pollution will accumulate at the same rate, but cannot be mitigated through roofing. When walking through polluted terrain in [caravans](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Caravan), they will accumulate buildup at 20% per day in Moderately Polluted terrain (50%-75% polluted tiles) and 40% per day in Extremely Polluted terrain (above 75%). Buildup from both pollution as well as toxic fallout can stack, meaning a pawn standing in polluted terrain while a toxic fallout can gain 80%/day.

This buildup severity gained is proportional to the [Toxic Resistance](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Toxic_Resistance) [stat](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Stat) \- thus, [humans](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Human) buildup at the full rate, [animals](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Animal) at half, and [insects](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Insects) and [mechanoids](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Mechanoids) are immune.\[Body Size?\] Buildup can also be reduced through a variety of of other means including items such as the [gas mask](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Gas_mask) , [detoxifier kidney](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Detoxifier_kidney) , and [detoxifier lung](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Detoxifier_lung) , as well as [genes](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Genes) such as [Partial antitoxic lungs](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Partial_antitoxic_lungs) and [Total antitoxic lungs](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Total_antitoxic_lungs), or [Tox resistance](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Tox_resistance) and [Tox immunity](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Tox_immunity).

There is, effectively, a multiplier for each pawn on their toxic buildup accumulation from these indirect sources. This can vary between 15% faster buildup or 15% slower buildup than the average rate. There is no stat in the information panel to convey this variation, but it is static for a given pawn. This means that baseliner humans standing in pollution and a toxic fallout, with no Tox Resistance of any sort, can vary from either 68% to 92% buildup per day, rather than a constant 80%.

The buildup severity occurs in stages. The later stages cause permanent [dementia](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Ailments#Dementia) which does not wear off even after toxic buildup subsides. When a pawn affected by toxic buildup [dies](https://rimworldwiki.com/wiki/Death), there is a chance that its corpse will instantly rot \- this chance is equal to the severity.

Once a colonist returns to a safe area, such as a roofed area for toxic fallout, or otherwise stops taking severity increases, their buildup severity will gradually decrease. Once no longer exposed, severity reduces by 8% per day, meaning that it will take up to 12.5 days to eliminate all toxins accumulated in the body. However, colonists that reach high stages of toxic buildup are likely to develop further complications.
